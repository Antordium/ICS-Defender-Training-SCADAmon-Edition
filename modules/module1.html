üè≠ 1. What is ICS?

Industrial Control Systems (ICS) are the computers and networks that monitor and control physical processes in critical infrastructure. Unlike IT systems that process data, ICS systems control the physical world ‚Äì opening valves, starting pumps, generating electricity, and treating water.

ICS is found in nearly every critical infrastructure sector: electric power, water and wastewater, oil and gas, chemical manufacturing, transportation, and more. When these systems are compromised, the consequences can be physical ‚Äì affecting safety, the environment, and human lives.

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Concept

Lower Purdue levels require MORE security, not less. Level 0-1 devices directly control physical processes ‚Äì their compromise can cause real-world harm. The IDMZ (Level 3.5) is the critical boundary between IT and OT networks.

‚öîÔ∏è 4. IT vs OT: Different Worlds

Information Technology (IT) and Operational Technology (OT) have fundamentally different priorities. Understanding these differences is crucial for effective ICS security.

Aspect IT Environment OT Environment
Priority #1 Confidentiality Safety / Availability

üí° Remember: SAIC vs CIA

IT follows CIA: Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability
OT follows SAIC: Safety, Availability, Integrity, Confidentiality

In OT, keeping the process running SAFELY is paramount. A power plant that's offline is as much a failure as one that's compromised.

üìú 5. Learning from History

Major ICS cyber incidents have demonstrated the real-world consequences of inadequate security. These events shape our understanding of threats and defenses.

üé¨
Video: ICS Attacks - A History
[V1.3 - assets/videos/m1_v3_ics_attacks.mp4]

Stuxnet (2010)

üéØ Target: Iranian Nuclear Facility

The first known cyber weapon designed specifically for ICS. Stuxnet targeted Siemens Step 7 PLCs controlling uranium enrichment centrifuges, causing them to spin at damaging speeds while reporting normal operations to operators.

Key Lesson: Air gaps can be crossed via removable media (USB).

Ukraine Power Grid (2015, 2016)

‚ö° Target: Electric Distribution

Attackers gained remote access to SCADA systems at three Ukrainian power distribution companies. They manually opened breakers, causing blackouts for approximately 230,000 customers. Systems were also wiped to complicate recovery.

Key Lesson: Remote access can be weaponized; have manual backups.

Oldsmar Water Treatment (2021)

üíß Target: Water Treatment

An attacker remotely accessed the HMI at a Florida water treatment plant and attempted to increase sodium hydroxide (lye) levels from 100 ppm to 11,100 ppm ‚Äì potentially dangerous to public health. An alert operator noticed and reversed the change.

Key Lesson: HMI monitoring and quick operator response are critical.

Colonial Pipeline (2021)

üõ¢Ô∏è Target: Fuel Pipeline

Ransomware attack on IT systems led to the precautionary shutdown of OT systems controlling the largest fuel pipeline on the US East Coast. Result: Fuel shortages, panic buying, and $4.4 million ransom paid.

Key Lesson: IT attacks can force OT shutdowns; segment networks.
